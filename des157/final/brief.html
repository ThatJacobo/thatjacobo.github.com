-Final Project Brief : Galeria de Ortega-

To further improve my ‘Every Image” project further, I wish to further develop the amount of options and interactions within my website. This would include more articles, scrolling gallery of images, and more information associated.

>Comparison 1 - Netflix:

While the streaming platform has more images in a compact space than I am hoping for, the aspect of a scrolling, horizontal series of images is what I hope to conceive in my project. It has an easy to understand, interactable user interface that knows how to lead it’s customers to where they need to go using good visual hierarchy and excellent visual queues. I love more minimal/clean design elements, so how they display anything that is their own is extremely impressive to me. I find the overall experience of scrolling through what is comparable to my ideas as very satisfying. You can tell this was made by an excellent set of designers and coders.

You of course can find their site @ https://www.netflix.com

>Comparison 2 - JodieGG’s Portfolio:

The portfolio of my friend and fellow student, Jodie Gayagoy, proves as a good example of the type of content and interaction I strive to introduce into my website. Having images that can be clicked on for further information is the key to what I wish to do. This creates nice interactivity for the user that only leads to further knowledge of the content the audience is there for to begin with. Even using basic fading animations such as the banner she introduced into her design is beautiful and something I wish to take inspiration from when creating my piece. It has a beautiful yet simple monochromatic color scheme that I myself enjoy quite a lot on websites as you can tell from the one you are looking at now. The only part of this I don’t exactly enjoy is the fading text during scroll, as the images around it do not fade as well. I am guessing this is not possible to change because of the means of making it, but it annoys me nonetheless.

You can find this website @ https://jodiegg.info/work

>Goals

Overall, I want to create a website that will show off articles through thumbnail-like interactivity that can be scrolled through in a horizontal axis. Websites/applications such as the ones above are great examples of how I want to get this done. The Xbox interface has a clean rotating slideshow that has many clickable and automatic elements I wish to apply. Jodie’s website has a color scheme I wish to take inspiration from as well as articles that are easy to read and have a lot of specific, but readable info. While I want to make my UI have very little scrolling elements, the two websites are good inspiration otherwise.

>Design Strategies

I plan to keep my website looking similar in theme to the current studio 2. This would include spotlights with a black and white aesthetic with the exception of one color, as well as the hover, clicking, and scrolling design mechanics that were in place before. The only difference I can see is applying a new font, as my previous choice was purely because futura is what I default to when I have no time to try out the aesthetics of many fonts. I may also change the size of the overall User Interface, as things were either too big or too small in some cases mattering on your screen size. Getting the overall piece to look balanced no matter the screen may pose a challenge. Also, creating backgrounds for the articles may create a better unity with the home page as well as a better user experience to understand it is a popout. Overall there are many tweaks I can put in place to better my website’s experience.

>Interaction Strategies

As mentioned before, I wish to make the original, two-picture home page into a multiple-clickable-image, slideshow-esque, horizontal scrolling gallery. This may take some work to make look good, but getting easy-to-understand moving images similar in vein to a streaming site but on a larger scale is my goal. Alongside that, I hope to add some features I wasn’t able to in my website’s original creation such as the clickable images in the article’s slideshow that would display the image on a larger/proper scale, or the scrolling, image-changing slideshow. As I work I might add some surprise features, but these are what I strive to achieve.

>Experience Goals

When a user enters my site, I want them to be greeted with a simple layout including images in a row with names attached alongside the title “Galleria de Ortega” or Gallery of Ortega. I will have some pointing arrows on the edges of the shown images that will alert the user of more images that can be found by pressing the arrow. In doing so, the user should hopefully mouse over an image which would light up with a spotlight. In doing so, I hope that the user would figure out to click the image because of this interactivity and open an article. The article will have an image with a description on the right and an article that can be read on the left. The article will be slightly big enough to alert the audience that it can be scrolled down on. If the audience scrolls, it will display a separate image that coincides with the article. Regardless of their placement on the page, there will always be an ‘X’ to always show the audience the exit of the page.
